# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in tennetctl a welcoming experience for everyone, regardless of background, experience level, or identity.

---

## Our Standards

Behaviour that contributes to a good environment:

- Being direct and constructive — critique code and ideas, not people
- Asking questions openly — there are no stupid questions asked in good faith
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them get oriented
- Accepting feedback gracefully and explaining disagreements calmly

Behaviour that is not acceptable:

- Personal attacks, insults, or dismissive language
- Harassment of any kind, public or private
- Publishing someone's private information without permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions or reviews
- Bad-faith engagement — trolling, deliberate misrepresentation

---

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, report it by opening a private GitHub issue or contacting the maintainer directly.

The maintainer will review reports promptly. Anyone found to be behaving unacceptably may have their contributions removed and be blocked from further participation.
